Memphis Warehouse Pay Update

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the upcoming pay structure change.

🔍
1. Why is the company changing the pay structure?

We want pay to be fair, consistent, and easier to understand. The new structure helps explain how pay is decided and how employees can grow and earn more over time.

2. Is anyone's pay going down?

No. No employee's pay will be reduced as part of this update.

3. How was my pay reviewed?

Everyone was reviewed using the same criteria, including:

  • Attendance and reliability
  • Safety and following procedures
  • Quality, accuracy, and productivity
  • Teamwork, communication, and ownership
4. What is the starting pay for warehouse roles?

Starting pay is $17–$21 per hour, based on previous warehouse experience.

5. What are job levels?

Job levels help make pay decisions consistent and fair.

  • Most employees start in Level 1
  • Higher levels (Levels 2–4) are for employees who consistently perform well and take on more responsibility

Levels help guide pay and growth expectations.

6. Does moving up depend on how long I've worked here?

No. Moving up is not automatic and is not based only on tenure. It depends on performance, reliability, and business needs.

7. How can I earn more pay?

There are two main ways:

  • Strong, consistent performance over time
  • Extra skills or assignments, when the business needs them
8. What extra assignments pay more?

When the business needs these roles and the employee is trained and performing well:

Role Premium
Night shift+$0.50/hour
Forklift operator+$0.50/hour
Controls processing+$0.50/hour
Trainer+$1.00/hour
Lead+$1.00/hour

These premiums apply only while working in that role.

9. Are raises automatic every year?

No. Raises and level changes are based on performance and business need, not automatic timing.

10. What does "strong performance" mean?

Strong performance includes:

  • Showing up on time and being reliable
  • Working safely every day
  • Doing quality work with good accuracy
  • Being productive and completing work correctly
  • Being a good teammate and taking ownership
11. What if I want to move up or earn more?

Let your supervisor know you're interested. Focus on:

  • Reliability and safety
  • Quality and productivity
  • Teamwork and professionalism
  • Learning new skills when opportunities are available
12. When will pay changes show up?

If your pay is changing, you'll see it on the Thursday, February 12, 2026 payroll.

13. Can I talk to someone about my pay or growth?

Yes. You may request a one-on-one conversation with your supervisor to talk about your role, performance, or growth opportunities.

14. Will reviews still happen?

Yes. Regular reviews will continue as scheduled.

15. Who can I talk to if I still have questions?

Start with your supervisor. They can explain how the structure applies to your role and what growth looks like.
